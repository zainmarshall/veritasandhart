Logo Concepts

10 mark-focused directions. All use the green/gold palette.

1 — The Shield

Classic shield form with V and H separated by a gold vertical line. The shield means protection and trust. The gold divider is the "&" — the thing that holds them together.

Shield Mark

2 — Overlap

The V and H letterforms overlap and interlock. Where they share space, the forms merge — strategy and humanity occupying the same ground. Gold crossbar on the H.

Interlocking Monogram

3 — The Arch

Two columns joined by a gold arch — classical architecture that means structure, support, endurance. The arch can't stand without both columns. Neither value works alone.

Architectural Mark

4 — The Diamond

A rotated square (diamond) frames a gold ampersand. The diamond is a symbol of value, clarity, and precision. The "&" inside is the firm's identity distilled to its purest form.

Framed Ampersand

5 — The Scales

Scales of justice — Veritas literally means truth, and this is its oldest symbol. V and H sit in each pan, held in equal balance. Direct, legible, grounded in meaning.

Classical Symbol

6 — The Seal

Double-ring seal with V&H at center. Reads like a wax seal or institutional emblem — tradition, authority, something you'd stamp on a document you stand behind.

Seal Mark

7 — The Tree

Minimal branching tree — one trunk splits into two equal branches. Growth from a single root. The gold dot at the fork is the decision point where strategy meets care. The gold base line is the grounding.

Organic Mark

8 — The Compass

A compass rose — the firm helps organizations navigate change. The north arrow is direction and truth. Gold diagonals and center point add warmth. Means: we know where we're going.

Navigation Mark

9 — The Overlap

Two squares that intersect — where they overlap, gold appears. Two distinct entities (truth and heart, strategy and care) creating something new where they meet. The gold intersection is the value the firm creates.

Geometric Mark

10 — The Apex

Two converging lines meet at a gold point above a horizon. The upward convergence is two forces (truth and heart) arriving at the same destination. The horizon is the baseline — where the work happens. The gold apex is the outcome.

Convergence Mark
